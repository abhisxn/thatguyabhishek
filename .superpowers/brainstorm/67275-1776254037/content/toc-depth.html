Which heading levels should the ToC track?

Articles come from Notion. Headings map like this: heading_1 → H2 (major section), heading_2 → H3 (subsection), heading_3 → H4 (minor). How deep should the navigation go?

Example article structure

◉ Introduction
→ What is this about?
· Background context
◉ The Core Problem
→ Why it matters
◉ My Approach
→ Step 1
→ Step 2
◉ Takeaways
A

Top-level only (H2s)

Just major sections — clean, minimal sidebar. Best for shorter articles. No nesting.

B

Two levels (H2 + H3)

Major sections + subsections. Indented sub-items appear on hover or always visible. Sweet spot for most long-form articles.

C

Three levels (H2 + H3 + H4)

Full depth. Can get noisy on dense articles. Usually overkill unless writing a technical reference.
